Lesson 1 · Account Research

Know your buyer better than their own team does

Old Way — 45 min
  • • Google news, LinkedIn, 10-K
  • • Skim 6 tabs, copy-paste notes
  • • Manually build call prep doc
  • • Miss the competitor mention buried in a March press release
New Way — 90 seconds
  • • Drop account brief into Cowork
  • • "Prep me for my Ford call tomorrow"
  • • Get: 5 discovery questions, 3 news hooks, competitive risks, opening line
  • • Ready before your coffee cools
Demo Prompt
"I have a discovery call with James Okafor at Ford tomorrow. He's the Director of AI & Advanced Computing. They're trying to cut AV model training from 18 hours to 4. Write me a 3-minute call opener and my top 5 discovery questions."

Lesson 2 · Outreach

Lead with insight. Features come second.

Typical Cold Email

"Hi Priya, I'm Alex from NVIDIA. We help financial institutions accelerate AI with our H200 GPU platform. Our customers see 3x faster training times. Would you have 15 minutes next week?"

↳ Reply rate: ~2–3%
Insight-Led (Claude-Assisted)

"Hi Priya — saw JPMC's announcement to scale real-time AML monitoring 10x by Q3. Banks at that inflection usually hit a memory-wall on their training clusters before they hit a budget wall. Happy to share how two other Tier-1 banks solved it."

↳ Reply rate: 18–22%
Demo Prompt
"Write a cold email to Priya Nair at JPMorgan using the March 10 AML announcement as the hook. Lead with insight about scaling compute, not with our product. Keep it under 120 words. Give me 3 subject line options too."

Lesson 3 · Coaching + CRM

Your call notes → clean CRM + coaching in 60 seconds

Old Way — 25 min post-call
  • • Manually reconstruct what was said
  • • Guess at MEDDIC gaps you forgot to probe
  • • Write a "call went well" Salesforce note
  • • No coaching — you move to the next call
New Way — 60 seconds
  • • Paste raw voice-to-text notes into Cowork
  • • Get structured MEDDIC update, Salesforce summary, follow-up email
  • • Plus: 3 coaching observations — what you did well and what to improve
  • • Managers get quality updates without nagging
Demo Prompt
"Here are my raw notes from the Ford call. Write: 1) a Salesforce opportunity note, 2) an updated MEDDIC scorecard, 3) three coaching observations for me, and 4) my follow-up email to James with next steps."

Lesson 4 · Proposals

Write for the executive who has 90 seconds to decide

Old Way — 3 to 5 hours
  • • Pull last quarter's proposal template
  • • Swap company names, forget to change a few
  • • Spec-heavy, outcome-light
  • • Economic buyer skips to price and says no
New Way — 20 minutes
  • • Feed deal context into Cowork
  • • Get a 1-page exec summary written for Sarah Chen, VP-level, outcome-first
  • • ROI framed in their language: "$1.2B Azure AI revenue enabled"
  • • Handle objections before they come up
Demo Prompt
"Write a 1-page executive summary for Sarah Chen, VP at Microsoft Azure. She's non-technical. Focus on the $1.2B Azure AI revenue unlock and the 34% cost reduction. Address the multi-vendor / lock-in concern proactively."

Lesson 5 · Pipeline & Planning

Monday morning clarity — before your first coffee

Old Way — Sunday night dread
  • • Stare at Salesforce, don't know where to start
  • • Prioritize loudest deal, not most important
  • • Miss that one deal going dark
  • • Manager 1:1 is reactive, not strategic
New Way — 8 minutes Sunday
  • • Paste pipeline snapshot into Cowork
  • • Get priority ranking with reasoning
  • • Spot JPMorgan going dark (16 days silent)
  • • GTC strategy, Boeing close plan, manager update — all drafted
Demo Prompt
"Here's my pipeline for the week. Rank my deals by urgency, flag anything at risk, and draft a 5-bullet update for my Friday 1:1 with my manager. Also — how should I use GTC to advance my Ford and Boeing deals?"
