## Deal History (most recent first)

### Feb 18 — Email: Sent redlined contract

Sent Marcus the contract with our standard terms. He had flagged two items in the previous call: (1) the auto-renewal clause and (2) the data residency language. We accommodated the auto-renewal change but held on data residency — legal said it would set a precedent. Marcus acknowledged receipt, said he'd "run it by legal and get back to me." No response since.

### Feb 10 — Call: Legal review kickoff

45-minute call with Marcus and two people from their legal team (names not logged). Legal raised concerns about:
- Data residency (they need EU data to stay in EU — we technically have this but it's not in our standard terms)
- Indemnification clause (standard pushback, we've handled this before)
- Auto-renewal notification window (they want 90 days, we standard at 30)

Marcus seemed confident the legal concerns were solvable. He said "Diana has approved the budget — this is really just crossing t's." Next step: redlined contract within 3 days (completed Feb 18).

### Jan 28 — Call: Economic Buyer intro

First call with Diana Reyes (COO). 20 minutes. She asked pointed questions about ROI methodology and implementation timeline. I gave rough answers — didn't have the ROI deck ready. She seemed satisfied enough. Said "Marcus will drive this from here."

I don't feel confident I have a real relationship with Diana. Marcus said she's "bought in" but the call felt more like she was checking a box than genuinely championing it internally.

### Jan 15 — Demo: Full platform walkthrough

Demo with Marcus + his 2-person Sales Ops team. Went well — Marcus visibly engaged with the coaching analytics module. Identified integration with their Salesforce instance as a requirement (confirmed as standard). No technical blockers surfaced.

### Dec 19, 2025 — Call: Discovery

First discovery call with Marcus. Pain: top 20% of Thornwood's deal team generates 70% of portfolio company deal flow. COO is frustrated that the model depends on 2–3 senior people and isn't scalable. Marcus was tasked with finding a solution. He's done 2 other vendor demos (didn't name them). Timeline: "ideally before end of Q1."

## Open Issues

**On us to resolve:**
- Data residency clarification — legal says we support EU residency but it's not in standard contract terms; need custom language from our legal team
- Never sent the ROI deck Diana asked for on Jan 28

**On them to resolve:**
- Contract review — Marcus said "legal will get back to us" on Feb 18; 26 days of silence
- IT security review — has not been initiated; may not know it's a requirement

---

## My Assessment

I think this deal is alive but drifting. Marcus is our champion but he may not have the pull to move legal independently. Diana is the real decision-maker and I have a thin relationship there. The ROI methodology gap from our Jan 28 call may be lingering — she asked a direct question and got a vague answer.

The deal could slip to Q2 or stall indefinitely if legal decides the data residency issue is a blocker and neither Marcus nor I have a path to escalate it.

I haven't followed up in 26 days because I don't want to be pushy. That was probably a mistake.
