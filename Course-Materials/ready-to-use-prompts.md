# 20 Ready-to-Use Sales Prompts

Copy, customize, and paste these into Claude Projects chat or Cowork. Replace the bracketed placeholders with your real context. Apply the CRISP framework to expand any prompt that produces generic output.

---

## Account Research

**1. One-page account brief**
```
Create a one-page account brief for [Company] covering: business model and revenue model,
key executives (name, title, LinkedIn if available), recent news in the last 90 days,
likely pain points given what we know about [your industry/ICP], and a suggested
insight-led opening talk track for a first call. Flag any assumptions you're making.
```

**2. Trigger event scan**
```
Research [Company]'s last 90 days — funding rounds, leadership changes, product launches,
geographic expansion, earnings announcements, or press releases.
Format as a table: Trigger Event | Date | Why This Matters to Us | Suggested Opening Line.
```

**3. Buying committee map**
```
Identify all executives at [Company] who would influence a purchase decision for
[your solution category]. For each person, describe:
- Their likely priorities and KPIs
- Their probable objection to our solution
- The question I should ask them specifically
```

## Cold Outreach

**4. Single cold email**
```
Write a cold email to [Name], [Title] at [Company], referencing [specific trigger event].
Open with the trigger — show you understand what it means for their business.
Connect to the problem we solve for companies going through this.
Under 150 words. CTA: 15-minute call. Tone: direct and consultative, no jargon.
No "Hope this finds you well" or "I wanted to reach out."
```

**5. 3-email cold sequence**
```
Write a 3-email sequence for [Company] over 12 days.
Email 1 (Day 1): Cold intro with specific trigger. 120 words max.
Email 2 (Day 5): Different angle — reference a specific piece of their content or a
relevant industry trend. 100 words max.
Email 3 (Day 12): Soft breakup with a genuine, open-ended question. 60 words max.
Each email should feel standalone, not like part of a chain. Include subject lines.
Also write a LinkedIn connection request under 300 characters that matches the tone.
```

**6. Personalization Audit**
```
Analyze these [N] cold emails I've sent. For each one, score it 1–5 on:
- Specificity of the trigger referenced (1 = generic observation, 5 = highly specific event)
- Quality of the problem hypothesis (1 = feature push, 5 = genuine business insight)
- CTA quality (1 = vague "let me know", 5 = specific, low-friction ask)
Then: what patterns do you see across all of them? What would the top 10% of reps do
differently? Give me 3 specific rules to apply to my next batch of outreach.
```

## Discovery Call Prep

**7. Discovery questions from account brief**
```
Based on this account brief for [Company], generate 5 discovery questions using this funnel:
2 current state questions (understand their situation today)
2 consequence questions (understand the business impact of that situation)
1 change plan question (understand their motivation to act now vs. later)
Every question must reference a specific detail from their recent news or company profile.
No generic "what keeps you up at night" questions.
```

**8. Objection prep**
```
Prepare me for the 5 most likely objections in a call with [Company/Title/Industry].
For each objection:
1. The exact words they're likely to use
2. What's usually underneath the objection (the real concern)
3. A response that acknowledges the concern before addressing it
4. A question to test whether the objection is real or a deflection
```

**9. Discovery roleplay**
```
Act as a skeptical [Title] at [Company]. [Paste 3–5 bullets of context from account brief.]
You've agreed to a 20-minute call but you're evaluating 3 vendors and don't love being sold to.
Push back on generic questions. Engage genuinely with good ones.
After I say "end call," score my: opener (1–5), question quality (1–5), listening ratio (1–5),
next step close (1–5). One coaching note: the single most important thing to improve.
```

## Post-Call

**10. Full post-call processing**
```
Here are my notes/transcript from my call with [Company]:
[paste notes]

Deliver:
1. Structured call summary: pain points (with business impact), buying signals, objections
   raised and how handled, confirmed next steps, deal risks and unanswered questions
2. CRM update fields: recommended stage, probability change, next activity date, opportunity
   description update
3. Follow-up email draft: confirms discussion, references their exact words, confirms next step
4. Coaching note: one thing I did well, one specific thing to improve next call
```

**11. 360° Call Debrief**
```
Analyze this discovery call transcript against the Self-Coaching Scorecard:
[paste transcript]

Score each of the 7 dimensions 1–5 with specific evidence from the transcript:
Up-front contract, Insight-led opener, Question quality, Business impact uncovered,
Stakeholder mapping, Next step close, Talk-to-listen ratio.

Identify: 3 moments where a question would have been stronger than a statement.
Identify: whether the next step set was committed or vague.
Calculate: estimated talk-to-listen ratio.
One coaching note: the single most important thing to change in the next call.
```

**12. Pain and signal extraction**
```
Extract from this call transcript:
- Stated pain points (their words, not mine)
- Buying signals (moments of interest or urgency)
- Objections raised + how they were handled
- Committed next steps (with owner and date if mentioned)
- Deal risks and questions I have not yet answered for this buyer
- Key quote: the single most memorable thing the prospect said
```

## Proposals

**13. Executive summary from call notes**
```
Read this RFP: [attach or paste]
Read these call notes: [attach or paste]

Draft an executive summary for our proposal that:
- Speaks to their stated priorities in their own language (not ours)
- References their decision criteria from the RFP
- Opens with the business outcome they're trying to achieve — not with our company intro
Under 300 words. No boilerplate.
```

**14. Business case section**
```
Using these call notes, build a business case section for our proposal to [Company].
Quantify the ROI using the numbers they gave us in the call — don't invent figures.
If a number was estimated or implied, label it as an estimate.
Structure: Current cost of the problem → Projected impact of our solution →
Conservative, base, and upside scenarios → Payback period.
```

## Pipeline and Deal Coaching

**15. Deal risk analysis**
```
Review my CRM notes for [Company]: [paste notes]
What are the 3 biggest risks to this deal closing this quarter?
For each risk: what's the signal, what's probably driving it, and what's my recommended
next move to de-risk it?
```

**16. Stalled deal diagnosis**
```
This deal has been stuck at [stage] for [X weeks]. Here's everything I know:
[Paste CRM notes, email thread summary, call summaries]

Tell me:
1. What is likely happening on their side right now that explains the stall?
2. What questions have I not yet answered for the buyer?
3. What's the most probable path to getting this deal moving?
4. What is one specific action I should take in the next 48 hours?
```

**17. Email thread analysis**
```
Review this email thread with my champion at [Company]: [paste thread]
Where is the relationship strong? Where might there be risk I'm not seeing?
What is the champion's confidence level based on their language?
What should I ask them in my next message?
```

## Competitive

**18. Competitive battle card**
```
Create a battle card comparing us vs. [Competitor] from the perspective of a CRO
evaluating both solutions. Focus on the 4–5 criteria that matter most at the CRO level
(not feature lists). Structure as:
- Where we win (and why — specific, not generic)
- Where they win (be honest — buyers respect accuracy)
- Landmines (the questions I should ask to surface our advantages)
- Their likely attack on us (and my response)
```

**19. Competitive objection handling**
```
Help me handle this objection: the prospect says [Competitor] offers [feature/capability/price]
that we don't. Give me 3 different responses:
1. A redirect to what matters more than that feature
2. An acknowledgment + honest positioning of our difference
3. A question that surfaces whether that feature is actually a requirement or a nice-to-have
```

**20. Competitor intelligence scan**
```
Research [Competitor]'s recent activity: product announcements, pricing changes, executive
hires or departures, customer wins/losses, strategy shifts (blog posts, earnings calls,
LinkedIn posts from their leadership).
Summarize: what changed in the last 90 days, what it signals about their direction,
and how I should adjust my positioning when I encounter them.
```
