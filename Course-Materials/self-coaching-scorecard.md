# Self-Coaching Scorecard

Use this after every discovery call — first without Claude (honest self-assessment), then with Claude analyzing the transcript. The gap between your self-score and Claude's score is where you learn fastest.

---

## The Seven Dimensions

| Dimension | 1 — Needs Work | 3 — Developing | 5 — Excellent |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Up-front contract** | No agenda or expectations set | Partial agenda mentioned | Clear agenda, time, and next-step criteria set upfront before the conversation started |
| **Insight-led opener** | Generic intro or feature pitch — started with "tell me about your business" | Some context shown, generic problem framing | Specific business perspective that demonstrates research — showed you understand their situation before asking them to explain it |
| **Question quality** | Feature-focused or yes/no questions only | Mix of open and closed, some depth | Business impact questions that uncover real consequences — funnel from current state → impact → change plan |
| **Business impact uncovered** | Pain identified but not quantified — "it's a problem" | Some quantification, rough numbers | Clear link between pain and measurable business cost — specific dollar, time, or headcount figure the buyer confirmed |
| **Stakeholder mapping** | Only spoke to one person, no visibility to committee | Aware of others but no concrete plan to reach them | Full buying committee mapped with a specific plan to engage each: champion, EB, technical, legal |
| **Next step close** | Vague follow-up — "I'll send an email" or "let's stay in touch" | Next step agreed but not calendared live | Committed next meeting booked live on the call with date, time, agenda, and both calendars confirmed before hanging up |
| **Talk-to-listen ratio** | Rep talked >70% of the call | Near 50/50 split | Buyer talked >60% during the exploration phase — rep was driving through questions, not presenting |

---

## Scoring Guide

**28–35 (80%+)**: Excellent call. Look for the one dimension that scored lowest — that's your coaching edge.

**21–27 (60–79%)**: Solid foundation with visible gaps. Focus on the two lowest dimensions.

**14–20 (40–59%)**: Several areas need attention. Prioritize Business Impact and Next Step Close — these have the highest deal impact.

**Below 14**: Review the Orientation → Exploration → Advancement framework. The fundamentals need rebuilding before the refinements matter.

---

## The 360° Debrief Process

1. **Self-score first** (5 min, no AI) — score each dimension honestly before Claude sees the transcript
2. **Run Claude analysis** — use `/post-call [account] debrief` or paste transcript with:
   ```
   Analyze this discovery call transcript against the Self-Coaching Scorecard.
   Score each of the 7 dimensions 1–5 with specific evidence from the transcript.
   Calculate the talk-to-listen ratio.
   Identify 3 moments where a question would have been stronger than a statement.
   Identify whether the next step close was committed or vague.
   One coaching note: the single most important thing to improve in the next call.
   ```
3. **Compare scores** — where did Claude see something you missed? Where did your instinct beat the AI?
4. **Set one goal** for the next call — not seven. One specific behavior to change.
