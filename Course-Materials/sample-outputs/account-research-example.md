### Likely Pain Points

**1. Tacit knowledge trapped in top performers**
With 40 SDRs and a clear 70/30 distribution (top 20% generate 70% of pipeline — confirmed in your prior notes), Vantage's problem isn't hiring — it's replication. Their best SDRs have call instincts, objection reflexes, and persona fluency that isn't in any playbook. New hires are watching Gong recordings without a framework for extracting what made the call work.

**2. Ramp time outpacing their hiring velocity**
8 new SDR hires in a quarter means 8 people at 30–40% productivity for 90+ days. At their average SDR quota, that's roughly 6–8 meetings per SDR per month being left on the table during ramp. At their conversion rate, that's pipeline leakage they can quantify.

**3. Playbooks that live in Confluence, not in reps' workflows**
Gong is implemented but not scaled (confirmed: Marcus mentioned in a prior call that "Gong is basically a recording tool for us right now"). Confluence playbooks are the backup they know doesn't work. The knowledge exists — it's just not surfacing at the moment of need.

### Catalyst Fit

Our platform surfaces best-practice call moments, objection responses, and persona-specific talk tracks at the moment a rep needs them — during the call or in prep. For Vantage, the most direct value story is: cut SDR ramp from 90 days to 45 days, and replicate the top 20%'s instincts across the full team.

Reference customer to mention: **Keystone Manufacturing** — similar SDR/AE structure, documented ramp improvement from 94 days → 47 days after 90-day deployment.

### Exploration Questions (O → E → A)

**Orientation** (earn credibility before probing):
- "Your Gong implementation — is that being used actively for coaching, or is it more of a recording archive right now?"
- "When a new SDR joins, what does their ramp actually look like? What's the first 30 days?"

**Exploration** (current state → business impact → what would change):
- "You mentioned the 70/30 distribution in our last call. Do you have a sense of what the top 20% are doing differently — or is that part of what's frustrating?"
- "What does a deal look like that an experienced SDR closes vs. one a new hire loses? What's different at the point of failure?"
- "If that gap narrowed — if your median SDR performed at 70% of your top performer instead of 40% — what does that do to your Q3 pipeline number?"

**Advancement** (summarize, set next step live):
- "Based on what you've shared, it sounds like the core problem is that your best SDRs' instincts are in their heads, not in your playbooks — and that's compounding every time you hire. Is that how you'd characterize it?"
- "If this goes well, what does a decision process look like on your side — who else would need to be involved, and what timeline are you working against?"
