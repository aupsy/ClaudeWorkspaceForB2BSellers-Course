# Discovery Call Transcript
**Account**: Vantage Software
**Date**: 2026-03-12
**Duration**: 31 minutes
**Participants**:
- Jordan Rivera (Rep, Your Company)
- Marcus Reid (CRO, Vantage Software)

*[This is a sample transcript for course practice. Company and individuals are fictional.]*

---

**[0:00]**
**Jordan**: Marcus, thanks for the time. I saw on LinkedIn you just hit your 6-month mark at Vantage — congrats on the new role. How has it been?

**Marcus**: Honestly, it's been a bit of a fire drill. I came in knowing the pipeline was thin, but I didn't realize how uneven the team was until I started looking at the numbers. I have some really strong SDRs and some who are barely keeping up. The spread is wider than I expected.

**Jordan**: How wide are we talking?

**Marcus**: Top quintile — top twenty percent of my SDRs — generates about seventy percent of the qualified pipeline. The other eighty percent are generating thirty percent. I can't scale a business on that spread.

**Jordan**: Is that a talent issue or a process issue?

**Marcus**: I think it's mostly process. The top performers have figured something out that the others haven't. But if you asked me to describe what they do differently, I couldn't give you a crisp answer. It's tacit knowledge that's sitting in people's heads rather than being systematically transferred.

**Jordan**: So the core problem is making that tacit knowledge explicit and getting it to the people who need it?

**Marcus**: Exactly. And it's not just about training — training is a one-time event. I need something that's available in the workflow, in the moment when a rep is about to send an email or make a call. Not something they had to memorize from an onboarding session three months ago.

**[10:00]**
**Jordan**: What does your current stack look like on the sales coaching and enablement side?

**Marcus**: We use Outreach for sequencing. Salesforce as the CRM. We have a Confluence wiki with some playbooks that nobody reads. And we use Gong for call recording — which I love for my own coaching sessions, but it doesn't scale. I can review maybe three or four calls a week. I have forty SDRs.

**Jordan**: So you have good data, but no way to act on it systematically across forty people.

**Marcus**: That's exactly it. I can tell you which reps are underperforming from Gong and Outreach data. I just can't coach them all at once.

**Jordan**: When you imagine the ideal state — what does it look like? What's different?

**Marcus**: My SDRs get real-time guidance that's actually relevant to what they're doing right now. Not generic tips — specific suggestions based on the prospect they're researching, the email they're about to send, the objection they just heard. And I can see which reps are using the guidance and whether it's moving the needle, so I'm coaching the exceptions, not the rule.

**[18:00]**
**Jordan**: Let me show you how we'd approach that. *(describes capability)* The system surfaces guidance based on the context of the deal — who the prospect is, what stage they're in, what similar deals have done. Your top performers' patterns get encoded and distributed to the whole team.

**Marcus**: Does the rep have to go looking for the guidance, or does it surface automatically?

**Jordan**: It surfaces automatically based on what they're doing — in their Salesforce workflow, in their email client, in the Outreach sequence view.

**Marcus**: That's the key thing. If they have to go find it, they won't. The best SDRs don't need it, and the ones who do need it most will never look.

**Jordan**: Exactly. We've seen teams reduce the productivity gap between top and bottom performers by thirty to forty percent within ninety days.

**Marcus**: *(pause)* That's meaningful. If I could compress that quintile spread — even partially — the pipeline impact would be significant. We're trying to get to forty million ARR by end of year and we're currently pacing behind that.

**Jordan**: What does a proof of concept look like for you? Is that something you'd be open to?

**Marcus**: Yes, actually. If this does what you're describing, I'd want to run a quick POC with maybe three or four SDRs — a mix of high and low performers — and see if there's a measurable lift. I'd want to see results within thirty days.

**Jordan**: That's actually our standard pilot structure. Three to five reps, thirty days, we measure sequence open rates, call connect rates, and qualified meeting conversion. You'd have data at thirty days.

**Marcus**: Good. I don't like buying on faith.

**[25:00]**
**Jordan**: One thing I want to make sure I understand — what would a successful outcome look like for you personally? Not just the business metrics, but for you as a CRO.

**Marcus**: *(thinks)* Honestly? I want to be able to say in my next board review that I've identified and solved the productivity problem. Right now I have a diagnosis. I don't have a solution. That's uncomfortable.

**Jordan**: Board meeting — when's the next one?

**Marcus**: End of Q2. June.

**Jordan**: So if we started a POC next month, you'd have thirty days of data well before June.

**Marcus**: That's not a coincidence, is it? *(laughs)*

**Jordan**: If this works the way you're describing, I'd want to move by end of quarter. Is that realistic on your side?

**Marcus**: If the POC data is strong — yes. I have signing authority up to a certain level, and I think our deal would be in that range. So no legal department delays if we move fast.

**Jordan**: Perfect. Last question — who else would be looped in on the POC and a final decision?

**Marcus**: My VP of RevOps — Priya Sharma — would need to approve the technical integration. That's usually straightforward with Salesforce and Outreach. And HR would be cc'd on anything related to coaching or performance, just to keep them informed. But neither of them is a blocker — this is my call.

**Jordan**: That's helpful. So the POC decision is yours, and the final contract decision is yours up to a threshold.

**Marcus**: Correct. Move fast, show results, and we can close this before June.

**[30:00]**
**Jordan**: I'll send you a POC proposal today — three SDRs, thirty days, the metrics we'd track, and what the rollout would look like if we move forward. Does that work?

**Marcus**: Yes. Send it to me and CC Priya.

**Jordan**: Will do. I'll have it to you by end of day. When's a good time to reconnect after you've reviewed it?

**Marcus**: Next week. Tuesday or Wednesday morning.

**Jordan**: I'll send a calendar invite for Tuesday at 10am. *(confirms)* Marcus, this has been a really useful conversation. I appreciate how specific you've been — it makes it much easier to build something that actually fits.

**Marcus**: I've wasted too much time on vendors who don't listen. Appreciate the directness.
