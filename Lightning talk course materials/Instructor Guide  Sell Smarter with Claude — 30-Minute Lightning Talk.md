# Instructor Guide: Sell Smarter with Claude — 30-Minute Lightning Talk

## Overview

This guide is designed for the facilitator delivering the *Sell Smarter with Claude* lightning talk. It maps directly to the 13-slide deck and gives you talk tracks, demo scripts, timing cues, transition phrases, and tips for handling common audience reactions. The session is teaching-led, not product-led — every section rewards the audience with a usable idea before mentioning the full course.

**Total runtime:** 30 minutes  
**Slides:** 13  
**Format:** Presenter-led with 3 live demos; no audience interactivity  
**Goal:** Leave attendees with 1–2 habits they can apply today, and a clear reason to enroll in the full 5-hour course

***

## Room & Tech Setup (Before You Start)

- **Claude.ai open in a browser tab** — logged in, Projects enabled, in a clean session (no prior chat history visible)
- **Second tab ready** with a sample account brief or call transcript you'll paste during demos (prep this ahead of time)
- **Slide deck open in a second window** so you can alt-tab without fumbling
- **Font size on browser:** bump Claude's response text to 125% so the back row can read it
- **Silence notifications** on your machine — nothing kills momentum like a Slack ping mid-demo
- **Optional:** A printed one-page cheat sheet of the 5 lessons + 3 demo prompts as a fallback

## Slide-by-Slide Talk Track

***

### Slide 1 — Title (1 min)

**Goal:** Create curiosity, establish credibility, set a teaching tone immediately.

**Talk track:**
> "In the next 30 minutes I'm going to teach you five things the best sellers I know changed about how they work — and show you exactly how Claude makes each one ridiculously fast to do. No theory. By the time you leave, you'll have at least one thing you can try before your next call."

**Do not say:** "This is a session about Claude" or "I'm going to show you an AI tool." The word *lesson* signals value. The word *tool* signals pitch.

**Transition:** Advance slide immediately after the opening line. Don't linger.

***

### Slide 2 — The Problem (2 min)

**Goal:** Make the audience nod. They need to feel *seen* before they'll trust you.

**Talk track:**
> "Here's what the data says. Sales reps spend — on average — only 28% of their week actually selling. The rest? CRM updates. Email drafts. Research before calls. Notes after calls. Searching for content. That's not a motivation problem. It's a workflow problem. And it's completely solvable."

**Pause after "completely solvable."** Let it land.

> "The sellers who are hitting 120% of quota aren't working harder — they've just stopped doing the slow parts manually."

**Transition phrase:** "So here's what this session is. Five lessons. Each one is a habit shift. Each one takes Claude about 30 seconds to execute."

***

### Slide 3 — How This Works (1 min)

**Goal:** Set the pedagogical contract. Audiences absorb more when they know the format upfront.

**Talk track:**
> "Every lesson follows the same pattern. I'll tell you the principle — something you probably already believe is important. Then I'll show you the old way you might be doing it. Then I'll run the new way live in Claude. It takes about 3 minutes per lesson, and you'll leave with the prompt."

**Key message:** You are a teacher right now, not a salesperson. Reinforce that explicitly.

**Transition phrase:** "Let's start with the one thing that separates great discovery calls from average ones before they even happen."

***

### Slide 4 — Lesson 1: Research (3 min)

**Goal:** Deliver a genuine wow moment. This is your first demo — it must land.

**The lesson framing:**
> "Lesson one: Know more about your buyer than they expect you to. That's not new advice. But the execution gap is enormous. Most reps do 5 minutes of LinkedIn stalking and call it prep. Top performers spend 45 minutes and still feel unprepared."

**Demo setup (say this before switching to Claude):**
> "Let me show you what Claude can do in under 90 seconds."

**Demo prompt to run live:**
```
I'm meeting tomorrow with [Name], [Title] at [Company].
Research them using what you know about this company,
their recent news, likely priorities for someone in this role,
and any strategic initiatives their company has announced.
Give me: 3 insight-led talking points, 2 questions that show
I've done my homework, and 1 potential business pain to probe.
```

**What to watch for:** The response will typically be 3–5 paragraphs. Scroll slowly. Point out the specificity: *"Notice it's pulling from what it knows about the company's industry, not generic talking points."*

**If the demo output is weak:** Have a pre-baked screenshot ready as backup. Say: *"Here's what this looks like on a real account I prepped last week."*

**Transition phrase:** "Okay — you're prepared. Now the question is: did your outreach email even get a reply?"

***

### Slide 5 — Lesson 2: Outreach (3 min)

**Goal:** Show the contrast between generic vs. insight-led email. This is the most visual lesson.

**The lesson framing:**
> "Lesson two: Lead with insight, not with features. The worst cold emails open with 'I wanted to reach out because...' The best ones open with something that makes the buyer think: how did they know that about us?"

**Point to the slide's left panel (the generic email example):**
> "This is the email in every VP's inbox every morning. It's about the sender. It mentions the company name once, as a mail merge. Delete."

**Point to the right panel (the prompt):**
> "Here's what you give Claude instead."

**Demo prompt to run live:**
```
Write a cold outreach email to [Name] at [Company].
Lead with a specific insight about something happening
at their company right now — a product launch, hire,
earnings news, or strategic announcement from the last 90 days.
Connect that insight to a business challenge it creates.
Then offer one sentence on how we help with exactly that challenge.
Call to action: 15-minute conversation, not a demo.
Keep it under 120 words.
```

**What to say while Claude types:**
> "Watch the opening line. If it starts with something specific about their world — not yours — that's the difference between a 3% reply rate and a 21% reply rate."

**Key teaching line:** *"Insight first. Feature second. Claude finds the news hook so you can focus on the conversation."*

**Transition phrase:** "You got the meeting. Now what happens in the room?"

***

### Slide 6 — Lesson 3: Discovery (3 min)

**Goal:** This is a no-demo slide — keep it tight and conversational. The value is the reframe.

**The lesson framing:**
> "Lesson three: Your discovery questions reveal your preparation level before you ask a single one. Buyers can tell in the first 10 minutes whether you've done the work or you're winging it."

**Walk through the Old Way / New Way on the slide:**
> "Old way: 'Tell me about your biggest challenges this year.' That's a lazy question. You're outsourcing your homework to the buyer.

> New way: Give Claude your account notes, the job description, the company's recent earnings call transcript — whatever you have — and ask it to generate 10 questions a *deeply informed* rep would ask. Not generic discovery questions. Questions that only make sense if you know this company."

**Quick illustrative prompt (don't run live — show it on screen):**
```
Here are my account notes on [Company]: [paste].
Generate 10 discovery questions a highly prepared sales rep
would ask the VP of Operations in a 30-minute intro call.
Questions should reference specific details from these notes
and probe for problems we can actually solve.
```

**Key teaching line:** *"The goal isn't to impress them. It's to make them feel understood. Claude gets you to that place in 2 minutes."*

**Transition phrase:** "The meeting went well. Now what do you do in the next 2 hours?"

***

### Slide 7 — Lesson 4: Post-Call (3 min)

**Goal:** Second major demo. This one typically gets the loudest reaction because it solves a pain every rep feels every day.

**The lesson framing:**
> "Lesson four: The follow-up email sent within 2 hours wins the deal. The one sent the next morning loses it to whoever followed up faster. The problem? Writing that email after a 45-minute call takes another 45 minutes if you're doing it right."

**Demo setup:**
> "Watch this. I'm going to paste in a call transcript — this is a real one I've anonymized — and ask Claude to do everything I normally spend an hour on."

**Demo prompt to run live:**
```
Here's a transcript from a discovery call I just finished: [paste transcript].

Please give me:
1. A follow-up email that opens by confirming I understood
   their 3 biggest pain points, then outlines clear next steps
2. A CRM summary: key pain points, budget signals, timeline,
   champion, decision process, and my recommended next action
3. 3 follow-up questions I should ask on the next call
   based on what they didn't fully answer today
```

**While Claude generates:**
> "This is normally 45–60 minutes of work. Watch how long this takes."

**Point out the CRM summary specifically:**
> "That section right there — that's what your manager is asking for in your next pipeline review. Done."

**Key teaching line:** *"Speed signals seriousness. The rep who follows up first with the most relevant email wins more than they lose."*

**Transition phrase:** "And that brings us to the one that affects your number the most: what's in your pipeline right now, and what are you doing about it?"

***

### Slide 8 — Lesson 5: Pipeline (3 min)

**Goal:** Connect all the lessons to revenue outcome. This is where reps start doing mental math.

**The lesson framing:**
> "Lesson five: Your pipeline is only as good as your last update. Most reps have 3–4 deals that have gone quiet. They know it. Their manager knows it. But figuring out what to do about each one takes energy they don't have at the end of the week."

**Walk through the slide content:**
> "Here's the habit. Every Monday morning, paste your stale deals into Claude — just the deal name, last contact date, stage, and what you know about the blocker. Ask Claude to diagnose what's wrong with each deal and recommend a specific re-engagement move for each one. Not a generic 'just checking in.' A specific, personalized message based on what that buyer cares about."

**Optionally show a quick prompt on screen:**
```
Here are 4 deals that have gone quiet in my pipeline: [paste].
For each one: diagnose the most likely reason for silence,
and write a re-engagement message tailored to what that
specific buyer cares about. Do not use generic check-in language.
```

**Key teaching line:** *"Your pipeline meeting becomes a conversation about strategy, not a status update."*

**Transition phrase:** "Now here's what I want you to notice about these five lessons together."

***

### Slide 9 — The Compound Effect (2 min)

**Goal:** Make the time savings concrete. Let the math do the work.

**Talk track:**
> "Each of these individually saves you 30 minutes. Run them all, every day, and you're recovering 2–3 hours. That's not a productivity hack. That's half a day back every single day. What would you do with 2 extra hours? More calls. More pipeline. More coaching time with your team."

**Point to the math on the slide. Let it sit for a moment.**

> "The best sellers I know aren't spending less time on selling. They're spending more — because they're no longer doing the work that Claude does better than they do anyway."

**Transition phrase:** "Here's exactly how to get started today — in about 15 minutes."

***

### Slide 10 — Quick Start (2 min)

**Goal:** Reduce friction to zero. Give them a specific, achievable action.

**Talk track:**
> "Four steps. Step one: go to Claude.ai and create your first Project — name it after your top account. Step two: paste in everything you know about that account: LinkedIn profiles, company news, your call notes. Step three: run the research prompt from Lesson 1. Step four: before your next call this week, run the discovery prep prompt. That's your homework."

**Emphasize:**
> "You don't need to do all five lessons at once. Do one. Prove it to yourself. Then add the next one."

**Transition phrase:** "And for those of you thinking — this is great, but I don't want to paste data into Claude every single time — there's a Level 2 that solves that entirely."

***

### Slide 11 — Level 2: MCP & Integrations (3 min)

**Goal:** Plant the seed for the full course. Make automation feel real and accessible, not technical.

**Talk track:**
> "Right now, everything we've done required you to paste something into Claude — a transcript, some notes, deal data. That's fine to start. But imagine if Claude already knew what was in your Salesforce pipeline. Already had your Gong call from this morning. Already saw your calendar for the week. No pasting. No copying. It just knows."

**Point to the MCP explainer box on the slide:**
> "That's what MCP enables. MCP stands for Model Context Protocol — but here's all you need to know: it's a way to give Claude a direct connection to the apps you already use. Think of it like USB for AI. Once you plug in Salesforce or Gmail or Gong, Claude can read from them and write back to them without you doing anything manually."

**Point to the apps list:**
> "Salesforce, HubSpot, Gmail, Gong, Slack, Notion — these all have connectors available today. We'll walk through the setup in the full course."

**Point to the Week 2 homework strip:**
> "If you want to try it on your own after today: start with the Gmail MCP or Salesforce MCP. Both have one-click installs. The full course shows you the exact setup, what to enable, and how to prompt Claude once it's connected."

**Transition phrase:** "Which brings me to where we go from here."

### Slide 13 — Closing (2 min)

**Goal:** End with action, not applause. The best lightning talks leave the room moving, not just impressed.

**Talk track:**
> "Don't try to apply all five lessons this week. Pick one. Just one. If your next call is tomorrow, run the research prompt tonight and see what it gives you. If you sent a cold email this morning that got no reply, rewrite it with the insight-led prompt and send a follow-up. One prompt. One habit. That's it."

## Handling Common Audience Reactions

### "What about data security / company policy?"

**Response:**
> "Great question — and the right one to ask. Claude.ai for Teams and Enterprise has data controls where your inputs are not used for training. But more practically: anything you'd put in a Google Doc or a shared Slack message, you can put in Claude. Don't paste proprietary contracts or M&A-sensitive information. For everything else — call notes, account research, email drafts — you're in the same risk territory as any cloud tool your company already approves."

### "Won't the emails sound AI-generated?"

**Response:**
> "That depends on how you use it. If you take Claude's output word-for-word and hit send, yes, it might sound generic. The workflow is: Claude drafts, you edit. You're removing the hard part — staring at a blank page — not removing yourself from the email. The best reps I've seen using this spend 5 minutes editing, not 30 minutes writing from scratch."

### "My company uses Salesforce / HubSpot / [tool] — does this work with that?"

**Response:**
> "Yes — and that's exactly what Slide 11 is about. Salesforce and HubSpot both have MCP connectors. We cover the setup in detail in the full course. In the meantime, the manual workflow today works great — you paste deal data in, Claude generates the output, you paste it back. The MCP setup just removes that middle step."

### "Can Claude actually access the internet / look up my prospects?"

**Response:**
> "Claude's knowledge has a training cutoff, so for very recent news you'd want to paste it in. What it's excellent at is synthesizing what it knows about companies, industries, and roles to generate highly relevant prep — and when you add current news from a quick Google search, the combination is genuinely powerful."

### "Is this replacing BDRs / AEs?"

**Response:**
> "No. Claude doesn't pick up the phone, build relationships, or read the room on a call. What it does is remove the administrative tax on everyone in those roles — so a BDR spends more time on conversations and less on copy-pasting. The reps who treat it as a threat lose. The ones who treat it as a force multiplier win."

## Pacing Red Flags

| If you notice this... | Do this |
|---|---|
| Audience checking phones during Slide 2 | Speed up — skip the second stat, get to the demo faster |
| Demo taking too long to generate | Narrate while it loads; if >30 seconds, switch to pre-baked screenshot |
| Questions coming before you've finished a lesson | Say "Hold that — I'll answer it at the end" and keep moving |
| Running 5+ minutes behind by Slide 7 | Skip Slide 8 talk track — point to the slide and say "same pattern, pipeline context" |
| Audience very engaged, asking lots of questions | Acknowledge it, defer all Q&A to end: "I love this — let's hold questions until the last 5 minutes so everyone gets the full picture" |

## After the Talk

**Within 24 hours:** Send a follow-up email to all attendees with:
1. The 5 lessons summarized in one sentence each
2. The 3 demo prompts (copy-paste ready)
3. The Quick Start 4-step instructions from Slide 10
4. The registration link for the full course

**For demand tracking:** Ask attendees to reply to the email with which lesson they tried first. This creates a natural follow-up conversation and surfaces intent signals for course enrollment.
