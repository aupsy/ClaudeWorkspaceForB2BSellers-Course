## Raw Voice-to-Text Notes (paste these in with the prompt)

okay so had the call with james whitfield at ford today, went pretty well i think. started about 5 minutes late because he had another meeting run over. 

he confirmed they're definitely moving forward with on-prem AI infrastructure, like it's not a question of if it's a question of when and who. their q3 training deadline is real — they have a board presentation in september where they need to show the autonomous driving model hitting a new accuracy benchmark.

i showed him the h200 cluster demo and he was genuinely impressed by the training speed numbers. he asked specifically about the jump from their current azure ndv4 setup and i gave him the 5x improvement number which i think landed well. he nodded a lot.

biggest thing that came up was the microsoft relationship. he said and i'm quoting here more or less "look we have a strategic relationship with microsoft and my CEO is going to ask why we're not just using azure for this" — so that's the main objection. i didn't handle it super well honestly, i kind of pivoted to talking about CUDA which felt like i was dodging the question. i should probably have addressed the microsoft angle more directly.

budget — he confirmed the 15 million is real and pre-approved. said the CFO David McClelland will need to sign off on anything over 10 million. i forgot to ask whether David has already been briefed on this initiative or if that's something James is going to do. that's a gap.

technical next step — he wants to do a proof of concept. he mentioned his lead ML engineer is a guy named Raj Patel and Raj would need to be the one to sign off on the technical architecture before James goes to David. i should get Raj involved next.

i asked about timeline and he said they need to make a vendor decision by end of april. so that's roughly 6 weeks. 

we agreed i'd send over the TCO comparison vs azure by end of week. he also asked if i could get a reference from a tier-1 automotive company. i said i'd check on whether i can share the BMW reference — need to confirm with our reference team.

oh also he mentioned almost in passing that they had a call with AMD last week. he didn't give details but said "they came in very aggressive on price." so AMD is definitely in the deal. 

next steps: send TCO doc by friday, set up a call with Raj Patel next week, confirm BMW reference availability, and probably need to loop in our solution architect for the technical architecture conversation.

ended on a really good note. james said "i'm excited about where this is going" which felt genuine.

## MEDDIC Framework (for scorecard output)
- **M** Metrics — What measurable outcome does the customer want?
- **E** Economic Buyer — Who controls the budget?
- **D** Decision Criteria — What criteria will they use to decide?
- **D** Decision Process — What steps, who's involved, what's the timeline?
- **I** Identify Pain — What's the compelling event or pain?
- **C** Champion — Who will sell for you when you're not in the room?
