1
Pre-Call Intelligence
"The reps who win show up knowing more than the buyer expects."
Sellers who reference a specific trigger — a hire, earnings call, reorg, or press release — get 3× more responses than those who lead with product. Preparation is the differentiator.
⏱ Old Way — 45 min before each call
  • 🔍 LinkedIn + company site + 8 browser tabs
  • 📰 Googling for recent news — mostly noise
  • 📋 Copy-pasting notes into a doc by hand
  • 😓 Running out of time; go in half-prepared
⚡ New Way — 90 seconds
Your Claude Cowork Prompt
"Research [Company] for my call today. Give me: their top strategic priority this quarter, a recent trigger event (news, hire, earnings), and 3 conversation hooks tied to our solution."
→ Arrives knowing what the buyer cares about right now.
2
Insight-Led Outreach
"Nobody replies to emails about you. They reply to emails about them."
The #1 reason cold emails fail: they open with the seller's product, not the buyer's world. An email that references something real about their business — a launch, a challenge, a strategic bet — earns 4–6× higher reply rates.
❌ Feature-First — 2% reply rate
"Hi [Name], I'd love to show you how our platform helps teams like yours save time and improve efficiency. Are you free for a quick 15-min call?"
↑ Sounds like every other email in their inbox.
✅ Insight-First — Live Demo
Your Claude Cowork Prompt
"Write a cold email to [Name], VP Sales at [Company]. They just [recent trigger]. Lead with an insight about how this impacts their team, then connect it to one problem we solve. Under 80 words. No fluff."
→ Feels like 30 min of homework in their inbox.
3
Post-Call Momentum
"The follow-up is where most deals are won — or quietly lost."
67% of deals stall because the seller fails to reinforce value after the call. A same-day follow-up that mirrors the buyer's exact words — not generic next steps — keeps the deal moving and separates you from every other rep they spoke to.
⏱ Old Way — 30–45 min post-call
  • 📝 Reconstruct notes 3 hours after the call
  • 💻 Manual CRM update — fields half-filled
  • ✉️ Generic "great to connect" follow-up email
  • 😬 Buyer forgets what made the call exciting
⚡ New Way — 3 minutes
Your Claude Cowork Prompt
"Here's my call transcript: [paste]. Extract key pain points, agreed next steps, and draft a follow-up email that quotes something specific they said. Format CRM notes in bullet form."
→ Buyer thinks: "This rep actually listened."
4
Bespoke Proposals
"A proposal isn't a brochure. It's a mirror that shows the buyer their own problem — solved."
Buyers reject proposals that feel generic. The ones that win reference the buyer's exact language from discovery, quantify their stated pain in their own words, and make "yes" feel obvious — not risky. Most reps send brochures. Top reps send mirrors.
⏱ Old Way — 3–4 hours
  • 📄 Copy last proposal, find-and-replace names
  • 🤔 Generic "why us" section that fits no one
  • 📊 ROI numbers not tied to what buyer said
  • 😬 Buyer reads a doc that could be for anyone
⚡ New Way — 45 minutes
Your Claude Cowork Prompt
"Using my discovery notes, write the executive summary for [Company]'s proposal. Mirror their language, quantify their stated priority of [X], and frame our solution as the lowest-risk path to that outcome."
→ Buyer thinks: "They actually understood us."
5
Pipeline Command
"Reactive sellers chase. Proactive sellers lead."
Top performers review their pipeline every Friday and arrive Monday with a clear offensive plan — knowing which deal needs a nudge, which is at risk, and which conversation needs a reframe. They don't respond to the week. They direct it.
⏱ Old Way — Monday morning chaos
  • 😤 9am: still figuring out priorities
  • 📬 Inbox triage eats the whole first hour
  • 🗓 Calls start before any prep is done
  • 🏃 Reactive all week — never in control
⚡ New Way — 8 min Friday 4pm
Your Claude Cowork Prompt
"Review my open pipeline. For each deal meeting this week: a 3-bullet prep brief with last interaction, key next step, and one risk to address. Flag the 2 deals most likely to slip and why."
→ Arrives Monday already ahead of the week.
