## The Problem This Course Solves

Sales reps spend roughly 65% of their time on activities that are not direct selling — research, CRM updates, writing follow-up emails, building proposals, and reviewing pipelines. But that statistic obscures a deeper problem: when reps do get in front of buyers, they often underperform because they didn't have time to prepare properly.[^2]

ATD research found that discovery and closing are the two weakest skills for most reps before structured training. The culprit is not capability — it is capacity. Reps who spend 45 minutes on manual Google research before every call simply have no time left to think deeply about their questions, their insight-led opener, or their personalization strategy.[^3]

Claude Cowork solves both problems simultaneously. After deploying Claude-powered tools across their 29,000-person workforce, ServiceNow sellers saw up to a **95% reduction in preparation time for customer meetings**. Teams using Claude-assisted email sequences have achieved a **52% open rate and 21% reply rate**, compared to industry averages of 15–20% open and 3–5% reply. Sales proposals can be generated **30–50% faster**, freeing reps to focus on discovery calls, negotiations, and relationship-building.[^4][^5][^6]

The freed-up time is not meant to be filled with more admin. It is meant to make the 35% of time that is direct selling dramatically better.

## What is Claude Cowork?

Claude Cowork is an agentic AI mode inside Claude Desktop that **plans and executes multi-step tasks, writing results directly into your file system**. Unlike standard AI chat — where you ask a question and get a text response — Cowork acts more like a diligent colleague: you assign a goal, it creates a visible step-by-step plan, you approve it, and it delivers finished files.[^7][^8]

Three capabilities make it fundamentally different from regular Claude chat:[^8]

- **File-native operation:** Claude reads, writes, and organizes files directly in a folder you designate — no manual copy-pasting between tools
- **Parallel sub-agents:** For larger tasks, Cowork splits work into multiple tracks running simultaneously, then merges results
- **Asynchronous execution:** You review the plan, approve it, and let Cowork run to completion while you focus elsewhere

Cowork also connects to external tools via MCP Connectors — including Google Drive, Slack, Gmail, HubSpot, Close CRM, and Salesforce — enabling multi-system workflows from a single prompt. A Sales plugin is included in Anthropic's open-sourced starter set.[^9][^10][^7]

**The three modes, in order of sophistication:**

| Mode | What It Does | When to Use |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Claude Chat | One-off questions and tasks; resets each session | Quick drafts, single questions |
| Claude Projects | Persistent memory — upload your ICP, templates, voice once; Claude never forgets | Recurring tasks that need your context |
| Claude Cowork | Plans and executes multi-step workflows; delivers finished files autonomously | Full workflows — research, outreach sequences, post-call processing |

## Module 1: The AI Seller Shift — Why the Best Reps Win Differently Now

**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Coaching Skill:** Insight-led selling — leading with perspective instead of product  
**AI Skill:** Claude setup + first account research

### Opening Coaching Truth (15 min)

Open with a provocation before a single piece of technology is mentioned:

> "By the time a buyer agrees to a call with you, they've already done 70% of their research. They don't need you to tell them what your product does. They need you to tell them something they don't already know about their own situation."

The era of the "discovery call as introduction" is over. Research on 2026 selling trends confirms that buyers increasingly want sellers who arrive with a point of view — a hypothesis about their business, grounded in evidence. Reps who open with "tell me about your business" signal that they didn't prepare.[^11][^12]

**The honest diagnosis:** Most reps know this. They want to lead with insight. They just don't have time to build the brief that makes an insightful opener possible.

**Opening activity — The Contrast:**
1. Ask a volunteer to give their usual opening line for a cold call with a target account
2. Pull up a Claude-generated account brief for that same account (prepared in advance by the instructor)
3. Ask the volunteer: "Now that you've read this — what would you open with instead?"
4. The difference between their two answers IS the lesson

This is the first "wow" moment. The brief doesn't just save time — it changes what you say and how you're received.

### The Time Math (10 min)

Frame the opportunity:

- Reps spend ~65% of time on non-selling activities[^2]
- Most of that time follows a **repeatable pattern** — same research format, same email structure, same follow-up flow every time
- Repeatable + pattern-based = perfect candidate for AI automation

The goal is not to do more tasks with the same effort. It is to recover time for the things AI cannot do: building genuine relationships, applying complex judgment, and having conversations that require real human presence.

### Claude 101 for Sellers (15 min)

Introduce the three modes (Chat → Projects → Cowork) using a progression the room can immediately grasp:

> "Chat is a smart intern who forgets everything after each conversation. Projects is a trained assistant who knows your territory, your ICP, and your voice. Cowork is an autonomous colleague who works while you sleep."

### Setup Workshop (20 min)

Every rep completes setup during this session. Instructor walks through each step live:

1. **Install Claude Desktop** (Mac or Windows)
2. **Sign up for Claude Pro ($20/month)**
3. **Create a Project: "My Territory"**
   - Upload: ICP definition (a rough paragraph works)
   - Upload: product/solution one-pager or key value props
   - Upload: 3 high-performing emails from yourself or top teammates
   - Set instructions: name, role, tone preferences, output format preferences
4. **First task:** "Create a one-page account brief for [Company] — cover business overview, key executives, recent news, likely pain points, and a suggested insight-led talk track."

### Module 1 Deliverable

One live account brief for a prospect currently in the rep's pipeline — with a rewritten insight-led opener based on what they learned.

## Module 2: Account Intelligence — Discovery Is a Research Problem

**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Coaching Skill:** The Orientation → Exploration → Advancement discovery framework  
**AI Skill:** Building a reusable account research Skill

### Opening Coaching Truth (15 min)

ATD's research found discovery and closing are the two weakest skills for most reps before training. And the reason discovery fails is almost never that reps don't know how to ask questions — it is that they arrive underprepared to ask the *right* questions.[^3]

**Teach the Orientation → Exploration → Advancement framework:**[^13]

- **Orientation:** Open with a credibility statement that earns the right to ask hard questions. "We work with a lot of companies in your space going through EMEA expansion. One thing we consistently see at this stage is X. I'm curious whether that's true for you." This is insight-led — it shows you understand their situation before asking them to explain it.
- **Exploration:** Use question funnels, not interrogation. Sequence: *Current state* → *Business impact of the current state* → *What would change if that problem went away?* Never jump straight to pain without earning it.
- **Advancement:** End every call by summarizing what you heard in *the buyer's exact words*, then set a next step with a date and an agenda — live on the call, before you hang up.

**The coaching realization:** Discovery quality is a research problem as much as a questioning technique problem. You cannot ask insightful questions about a business you don't understand. Claude solves the research half; this module teaches the questioning half.

### Building an Account Research Skill (25 min)

A Claude Skill is a reusable instruction set that Claude follows automatically. An Account Research Skill contains:[^6]

- Your ICP profile (company size, industry, tech stack, common pain points)
- The brief format (sections: company overview, key stakeholders, recent triggers, pain point hypothesis, discovery questions, opening talk track)
- Output length and formatting preferences
- Competitor context relevant to your solution

**The Skill also generates discovery questions aligned to the Exploration framework:**

```
Post-research, generate 5 discovery questions using the funnel:
1. Two Current State questions (understand their situation)
2. Two Consequence questions (understand the business impact)
3. One Change Plan question (understand their motivation to move)
Questions must reference specific details from the company's recent news or profile.
```

**Live build:** Instructor creates the SKILL.md file in real time using the team's own playbook as input.

### The SPEAR Framework (10 min)

Use SPEAR to identify which of your workflows belong in a Skill:[^6]

| Criteria | Question |
|----------|---------|
| **S — Standardized** | Does this task always start with the same type of input and end with the same type of output — regardless of the account? |
| **P — Prompt-heavy** | Would executing this task well require a lot of upfront explaining — your company, product, tone, rules — before anyone could do it properly? |
| **E — Error-prone** | Does manual execution introduce inconsistencies I wouldn't want a buyer to see? |
| **A — Accounting for time** | Does this consume 30+ minutes per rep per week? |
| **R — Reusable** | Would more than one person on the team use this? |

Any workflow scoring 3+ is worth building a Skill for.

**Facilitation note — running this with day-1 students:**

Students can self-score E, A, and R immediately — those are about their existing work habits, not their Claude experience. S and P are the ones that need a brief walk-through because the natural instinct is to answer them in terms of Claude usage ("I haven't used Claude enough to know").

Redirect with this framing before releasing students to score:

> *"For S: don't think about Claude yet — just think about the task itself. Does a post-call CRM update always look the same? Same inputs (call notes), same outputs (filled fields)? Yes. Does a one-off executive escalation email always look the same? No — every situation is different. That's the test."*
>
> *"For P: imagine explaining this task to a smart new hire on their first day. Cold email? You'd have to explain your ICP, your tone, which phrases to avoid, what CTA to use, how we position against competitors — a lot of context. That context is what makes it Prompt-heavy. Scheduling a meeting? Almost no explanation needed."*

**Suggested 2-minute facilitation sequence:**
1. Hand out (or point to) `Course-Materials/spear-framework.md`
2. Show the two pre-scored calibration examples at the top of the table — post-call CRM update (5/5) and one-off escalation email (0/5) — and ask: "Why does the CRM update score high on S and P? Why does the escalation email score zero?" Let 2–3 students answer.
3. Then say: "Now score 2–3 of your own workflows. Write your top candidate on paper — we'll come back to it in Module 5 to actually build the Skill."

The calibration step prevents the most common failure mode: students marking everything as a 1 because they're unsure, ending up with artificially high scores that don't actually guide Skill-building priority.

### The Discovery Roleplay (10 min)

**New activity:** After generating an account brief, each rep uses Claude as a practice sparring partner:

```
Act as a skeptical VP of Sales at [Company]. You've agreed to a 20-minute discovery call.
You're evaluating three vendors. You don't love being sold to.
Answer my discovery questions honestly but with appropriate resistance.
After I'm done, score my performance on: insight-led opener, question quality, 
whether I listened or just moved on, and quality of my next-step close.
```

This is private, non-judgmental practice — reps can fail and learn without an audience. It also produces a coaching note that feeds directly into their self-awareness.

### Module 2 Deliverable

A reusable account research Skill tested against 3 real accounts, plus one Claude-coached discovery roleplay with a written scorecard.

## Module 3: Outreach That Gets Responses — Insight Personalization vs. Surface Personalization

**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Coaching Skill:** The personalization paradox — why trust beats tactics  
**AI Skill:** Building a cold outreach Skill and multi-touch sequences

### Opening Coaching Truth (10 min)

Consultative selling research is consistent: buyers choose sellers they trust over sellers with the best product. Trust is built through demonstrated understanding — not charm, not persistence, not a clever subject line.[^14][^15]

**Teach the distinction between two types of personalization:**

- **Surface personalization:** "I saw you went to Michigan and root for the Wolverines!" — feels forced, signals shallow research, gets politely ignored
- **Insight personalization:** "I noticed you're expanding into EMEA right after a Series C — most sales orgs we work with hit a significant SDR ramp-time wall at exactly this moment. I'm curious if that's showing up for you." — builds trust because it demonstrates you understand their business situation specifically

The problem: insight personalization takes 45 minutes of research per prospect. Which is why most reps default to surface personalization or skip it entirely.

**The Personalization Audit (new activity):**
Have reps pull their last 10 sent cold emails. Ask Claude to analyze them:

```
Analyze these 10 cold emails I've sent. For each one, score it 1–5 on:
- Specificity of the trigger referenced (1 = generic, 5 = highly specific)
- Quality of the problem hypothesis (1 = feature push, 5 = genuine business insight)
- CTA quality (1 = vague "let me know", 5 = specific, low-friction ask)
Then tell me: what patterns do you see? What would the best rep in my company 
do differently?
```

The score usually stings. The gap creates intrinsic motivation to build the outreach Skill properly.

### The Anatomy of a High-Converting AI Email (10 min)

Demonstrate the difference between generic and contextualized prompting:

**Generic prompt:**
> "Write a cold email to the VP of Sales at Acme Corp about our sales automation software."

**Contextualized prompt (after running account research):**
> "Using the Acme account brief, write a cold email to [Name], their VP of Sales. Open with their Series C announcement as the trigger. Reference their likely challenge of scaling their SDR team fast without losing quality. Tie this to our 40% ramp time reduction stat. Under 150 words, conversational tone. CTA: 15-minute call. Add a P.S. referencing their recent conference presentation."

The difference in output is dramatic — and the lesson is about quality of context, not quality of prompting technique.

### Building the Cold Outreach Skill (20 min)

The outreach Skill contains:

- ICP parameters — who this email is for (seniority, function, industry)
- Tone and voice guidelines — formal vs. conversational, length constraints
- Email structure template — trigger → problem hypothesis → value prop → CTA → P.S.
- 3–5 high-performing email examples as reference anchors
- Banned phrases — "Hope this email finds you well," "I wanted to reach out," etc.
- CTA library — 5–7 proven options mapped to sales stage

**Critical instruction to add to the Skill:** "Every email must open with a specific, researched trigger event — not a generic observation. If no trigger is available, say so rather than inventing one."

### Multi-Touch Sequences and LinkedIn (10 min)

Claude writes full multi-touch sequences with built-in escalation logic:

```
Using the Acme account brief, write a 3-email sequence:
Email 1 (Day 1): Cold intro referencing Series C — 120 words max
Email 2 (Day 4): Different angle — reference their blog post on scaling — 100 words max
Email 3 (Day 10): Soft breakup email with a genuine question — 60 words max
Each email should feel like a standalone message, not a follow-up chain.
Also write a LinkedIn connection request (300 character limit) that sets the right tone.
```

### Module 3 Deliverable

A 3-email sequence and LinkedIn message for a real prospect, plus a completed Personalization Audit with a written reflection on what they'll change.

## Module 4: Pipeline Velocity — The Self-Coaching Loop

**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Coaching Skill:** Structured self-coaching — closing the feedback gap  
**AI Skill:** Post-call workflow, proposals, and deal coaching

### Opening Coaching Truth (10 min)

Sales coaching research shows reps improve fastest when feedback is specific, timely, and tied to actual conversations. The problem: most reps never get coached on their individual calls because managers don't have the capacity. This creates a **skill plateau** — reps repeat the same patterns indefinitely, improving slowly or not at all.[^16][^17]

Introduce the **Self-Coaching Scorecard** — a lightweight, consistent framework reps apply after every call:

| Question | What to Assess |
|----------|---------------|
| Did I set an up-front contract? | Did the buyer know the agenda, the time, and what happens next before we started? |
| Did I uncover business impact? | Did I get beyond "it's a problem" to "here's what it costs us"? |
| Did I map stakeholders? | Do I know who else influences this decision — and have I met them? |
| Did I earn the next step? | Did I set the next meeting live, with a date, agenda, and both calendars confirmed? |
| Talk-to-listen ratio | Did the buyer talk more than I did in the exploration phase? |

This scorecard works whether or not they use Claude. Claude makes applying it effortless.

### The 360° Call Debrief Activity (15 min)

This is the most powerful activity in the course. Take a real recent discovery call transcript (Gong, Chorus, Zoom, or rough notes) and run this process:

1. **Rep self-scores first** (5 min) — using the scorecard above, before Claude sees the transcript
2. **Run through Claude** (2 min):
```
Analyze this discovery call transcript against this scorecard:
[paste scorecard]
Score each item 1–5 with specific evidence from the transcript.
Also calculate the talk-to-listen ratio.
Identify: the 3 moments a question would have been stronger than a statement.
Identify: whether the next step set was committed or vague.
One coaching note: the single most important thing to improve in the next call.
```
3. **Compare** (8 min) — where did Claude see something the rep missed? Where did rep instinct beat the AI? Which gaps surprised them?

The debrief builds two things simultaneously: self-awareness (coaching skill) and calibrated trust in the tool (AI skill). Reps learn what Claude is good at and what still requires their judgment.

### Post-Call Processing Workflow (15 min)

The post-call Skill converts a raw transcript or rough notes into four outputs automatically:

```
Post-Call Processing Skill — trigger phrase: "Process my call with [Company]"

Deliver:
1. Structured call summary (pain points, buying signals, objections, confirmed next steps)
2. CRM update fields (stage change recommendation, close probability, next action date)
3. Follow-up email draft (references specific conversation moments, confirms next step)
4. Coaching note (one strength, one improvement, one question to ask next time)

Format each as a separate file in the /Calls folder.
```

**The time math:** This workflow takes 90 seconds to trigger and 5 minutes to deliver. The manual equivalent — update CRM, write follow-up email, organize notes — takes 60–90 minutes and is frequently skipped.[^16]

### Proposal Generation (10 min)

Feed an RFP or requirement document plus call notes into Cowork:

```
Task: Generate a first-draft proposal for Acme Corp

Inputs: [RFP file] + [call notes file]

Deliver:
- Executive summary tied to their stated priorities
- Solution sections mapped to their specific pain points from the discovery call
- Business case using the numbers they gave us in the call
- Implementation timeline
- Why us vs. alternatives they mentioned

Save as Acme_Proposal_Draft_v1.docx in /Proposals
```

Proposals are 30–50% faster with Claude. The coaching principle: a first draft that captures their language and priorities is more persuasive than a polished template that doesn't.[^4]

### Deal Coaching (10 min)

Claude can serve as an always-available deal coach — analyzing stalled deals and generating tactical recommendations:

```
I've been working on the [Company] deal for 8 weeks.
[Paste CRM notes, email thread, call summaries]

Tell me:
1. Top 3 risk factors for this deal not closing
2. Questions I have not yet answered for the buyer
3. What is the likely internal conversation happening on their side right now?
4. What should my next move be?
5. What is a realistic close path and timeline?
```

The coaching framing: "This is not Claude telling you what to do. This is Claude forcing you to articulate what you know and what you don't — which is what a good manager would do in a deal review."

### Module 4 Deliverable

A completed 360° Call Debrief with self-score vs. Claude score, plus a post-call summary and follow-up email from a real recent call.

## Module 5: The AI Seller Mindset — Amplifier, Not Crutch

**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Coaching Skill:** The de-skilling paradox and the new skills map for AI-era sellers  
**AI Skill:** Full Cowork configuration + CRM integration + autonomous workflows

### Opening Coaching Truth (15 min)

This is the most important coaching moment in the entire course. Name the risk directly:

> "Here is the danger: if you outsource all your thinking to Claude, you will gradually become less capable, not more. The reps who get the most out of AI tools in 2026 are the ones who use them to think harder, not to think less."[^18]

**The new skills map for AI-era sellers:**[^11][^18]

Research on 2026 sales trends reveals that the skills that create the most differentiation are shifting. The tasks AI handles well (research, drafting, data entry, summarizing) are table stakes. The tasks that create competitive advantage are becoming more human, not less:

- **Complex judgment:** Reading the room, identifying political dynamics in a buying committee, knowing when to push and when to back off
- **Genuine curiosity:** Asking questions because you actually want to know, not because a sequence triggered
- **Relationship depth:** The trust that develops over time through honest, interest-aligned conversations
- **Creative problem-solving:** Designing non-standard deal structures, finding the path to yes that no playbook anticipated

**Frame Claude Cowork accordingly:** The 65% of time currently lost to busywork should be reinvested in developing these skills — not in clearing more items off the task list.

**The "AI-Free" Debrief activity (15 min):**  
After the Cowork Monday morning workflow demo, pause. Have reps work in pairs, no devices:
- Tell your partner everything you know about your best deal right now — from memory
- What do you know for certain? What are you assuming? What haven't you actually asked?
- What is the buyer feeling that they haven't said out loud?

The contrast is revealing. The deal that was "prepped by Claude" often has shallow human understanding underneath. That gap is the work.

**Step 4: Connect CRM via MCP** (where available)

> "Which deals haven't had activity in 10 days? List them with last contact and recommended next step."

> "Summarize my open deals by stage and flag anything at risk of slipping this quarter."

### The Monday Morning Workflow (10 min)

Demonstrate an end-to-end Monday morning workflow triggered as a single Cowork task:

```
Task: Prepare my week for maximum selling time

Steps (run in parallel):
1. Review CRM pipeline → flag deals needing attention this week
2. For my 3 most important deals → create account briefs with latest news
3. Draft Monday outreach for 5 tagged prospects

5. Create a prioritized action list for today

Deliver each as a separate file. Run steps in parallel where possible.
```

Manual equivalent: 90–120 minutes. Cowork: 5–8 minutes while the rep prepares for their first call.

## Appendix A: The CRISP Prompt Framework

Every great Claude prompt includes five elements:

- **C — Context:** Who are you, what is the situation, what does Claude need to know?
- **R — Role:** What perspective should Claude take? ("Act as a senior enterprise AE...")
- **I — Instructions:** What exactly should Claude do, step by step?
- **S — Specifics:** Constraints, style rules, length, tone
- **F — Format:** What should the output look like?

**Example — generic prompt:**
> "Write a follow-up email to my prospect at Acme."

**Example — CRISP prompt:**
> "Context: 45-min discovery call with Maria Chen, VP Sales at Acme Corp. She mentioned their biggest pain is ramping new SDRs too slowly. Planning to hire 15 new SDRs in Q2. Uses Salesforce and Outreach. Next step: demo with her and the CRO next Thursday.  
> Role: Consultative enterprise AE who communicates clearly without jargon.  
> Instructions: Write a post-call follow-up that confirms discussion, reinforces our value prop on SDR ramp time, and confirms demo logistics.  
> Specifics: Under 200 words. Conversational prose, no bullet points. Reference the 15-SDR hire plan.  
> Format: Email with subject line, body, and professional sign-off."

## Appendix B: The Self-Coaching Scorecard

For use in the 360° Call Debrief activity and as a standalone weekly rep self-assessment.

| Dimension | 1 — Needs Work | 3 — Developing | 5 — Excellent |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| Up-front contract | No agenda or expectations set | Partial agenda mentioned | Clear agenda, time, and next-step criteria set upfront |
| Insight-led opener | Generic intro / feature pitch | Some context shown | Specific business perspective that demonstrates deep research |
| Question quality | Feature-focused or yes/no questions | Mix of open and closed | Business impact questions that uncover real consequences |
| Business impact uncovered | Pain identified but not quantified | Some quantification | Clear link between pain and measurable business cost |
| Stakeholder mapping | Only spoke to one person | Aware of others but no plan | Full committee mapped with entry strategy |
| Next step close | Vague follow-up or "I'll send an email" | Next step agreed but not calendared | Committed next meeting booked live, agenda confirmed |
| Talk-to-listen ratio | Rep talked >70% | Near 50/50 | Buyer talked >60% in exploration phase |

## Appendix D: Addressing Common Objections in Sales Team Training

**"This will make our emails sound robotic."**  
The opposite is true when done correctly. Run the Personalization Audit in Module 3 and let reps score their own emails first. The AI-assisted versions — when built on proper account research — are almost always *more specific and more insight-led* than what reps were writing manually. The robot feeling comes from generic prompts. This course teaches contextualized, research-fed prompting.[^5]

**"I already know how to sell."**  
Respect this. Name it directly: "This course is not for reps who need to learn to sell. It's for reps who are already good and want an unfair advantage." Then frame the de-skilling paradox honestly: the best sellers in 2026 will be the ones who use AI to execute their craft better — not the ones who either ignore it or let it do their thinking.[^18]

**"What about data security?"**  
Anthropic's enterprise and team plans have strong data handling commitments. For regulated industries, start with prompts that don't require uploading customer PII — focus on public company research and anonymized call notes first. When using MCP connectors for CRM, verify company data policy before connecting.

**"I don't have time to learn new tools."**  
The productivity math: setup takes 2 hours and saves 10 hours per week. Break-even is 1.2 weeks. After that, every week is a net gain. This objection usually reflects skepticism, not an actual time constraint — answer it with the rep's own time math from their territory.
