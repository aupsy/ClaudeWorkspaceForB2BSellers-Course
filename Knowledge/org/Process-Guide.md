# Process Guide

Practical guidance for the processes every rep needs to navigate.

---

## CRM Hygiene

**Why it matters**: Your pipeline is only as useful as your data. Managers forecast from this. Deal Desk approves pricing from this. Bad data = bad decisions.

**Required fields** (keep these current at all times):
- [ ] Close date (realistic, not aspirational)
- [ ] Stage (matches actual status)
- [ ] Amount (ARR, not TCV unless your org tracks TCV)
- [ ] Next step + next step date
- [ ] Economic buyer identified
- [ ] Competitors (tag any known competitors)

**Update frequency**:
- After every customer interaction: update next step and date
- Stage changes: same day
- Close date changes: flag to manager proactively — never let a date slip silently

**Deal hygiene red flags** (fix these before your pipeline review):
- Close date in the past with no update
- No activity in the last 30 days
- Stage: "Proposal" with no next step
- Amount: $0 or blank

## Opportunity Creation

**When to create**:
- After confirming the prospect has pain, budget, and a real timeline (after discovery)
- Do NOT create opportunities for every inbound lead — qualify first

**Required at creation**:
- Account, contact, stage (Discovery), amount (estimate), close date (estimate)
- Source (how did this come in?)

## Deal Desk Process

**What requires Deal Desk**:
- Pricing below [X]% of list
- Multi-year with non-standard escalators
- Free pilots or POCs beyond [X] days
- Payment terms beyond net-30
- Contract modifications to standard terms

**How to engage**:
1. [FILL IN — e.g., Fill out Deal Desk request form in Salesforce]
2. Required info: deal size, discount %, business justification, customer context
3. SLA: [FILL IN — e.g., 2 business days for standard, 5 for complex]

**Tips**:
- Submit early — don't wait until a customer is waiting for approval
- Include competitive context if applicable ("they have a competing proposal from [X]")
- Be specific with your business justification — "customer asked for it" will get rejected

## Legal & Contract Process

**Standard MSA**: Available in [location]. Pre-approved for deals up to $[X] without redlines.

**Customer paper** (using their contract):
- Submit to [legal@company.com / Legal portal]
- Lead time: [e.g., 5-7 business days]
- Attach: their contract + Salesforce opportunity link

**NDA process**: [e.g., Use standard NDA from Salesforce templates / DocuSign — can be sent same day]

**Common contract issues to watch for**:
- Auto-renewal language (ensure it's in or out per company policy)
- Limitation of liability caps
- IP ownership provisions (for professional services)
- SLA commitments that differ from standard

## Procurement Navigation

**When procurement gets involved**: Typically at proposal stage for deals over $[X]

**What procurement usually needs**:
- W-9 or tax forms
- Insurance certificates
- Vendor questionnaire (security, ESG, business continuity)
- Banking information

**Tips**:
- Ask about procurement early: "Once we agree on commercial terms, who from procurement would we be working with?"
- Build a relationship with their procurement contact, not just your champion
- Pre-fill the vendor portal in advance if you know the account is in your pipeline

## Handoff to Customer Success

**When**: At contract signature

**What CSM needs from you**:
- [ ] Account context: key stakeholders, their goals, what you sold them on
- [ ] Implementation complexity: integrations, data migration, customization needs
- [ ] Any commitments made during the sales process (especially non-standard ones)
- [ ] Champion and Economic Buyer contacts
- [ ] Any political dynamics or sensitivities

**Handoff format**: [e.g., Live handoff call, 30 min, within 1 week of signature]

**Golden rule**: Never make commitments during the sales process that you haven't cleared with CSM. The CSM relationship starts at the first customer interaction, not at signature.
