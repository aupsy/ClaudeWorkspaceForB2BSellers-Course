# Sales Plays

Proven plays for common situations. Adapt to your product and segment.

---

## Play 1: New Logo — Inbound Lead

**When to use**: Prospect fills out a form, requests a demo, or a sales-accepted lead comes in from marketing.

**Objective**: Book discovery call within 24 hours, qualify within first 2 calls.

**Steps**:
1. **Same-day outreach** (within 2 hours if possible): Personalized email + call. Reference what they downloaded/viewed.
2. **Discovery call** (within 48 hours): Run full MEDDIC discovery. No demo yet.
3. **Qualification gate**: After discovery, decide: qualified (move to demo) or disqualify. Don't nurture maybes — get a yes or no.
4. **Tailored demo**: Based on discovery, show only the relevant 3-4 use cases. Not a product tour.
5. **Proof**: Share a relevant case study or reference customer from their industry or use case.
6. **Proposal**: Custom proposal tied to their specific pain + metrics from discovery.
7. **Mutual close plan**: Agree on next steps to contract in writing.

**Success metric**: Discovery → Proposal in <3 weeks for mid-market.

## Play 2: Cold Outbound — New Prospect

**When to use**: Targeting a net-new account in your territory with no prior relationship.

**Objective**: Get a 30-minute discovery call.

**The sequence** (7 touches over 3 weeks):
1. **Day 1 — Email 1**: Personalized, specific to their role and company. 3-4 sentences max. One clear ask.
2. **Day 3 — LinkedIn connection**: With a brief note — no pitch. Just a connection.
3. **Day 5 — Call 1**: Leave a voicemail if no answer. Keep it to 20 seconds.
4. **Day 8 — Email 2**: Different angle — lead with a relevant customer story or insight.
5. **Day 12 — LinkedIn message**: Short message referencing their recent post or company news.
6. **Day 15 — Call 2**: Final call attempt. Reference email sequence.
7. **Day 18 — Email 3 (breakup)**: "I don't want to keep bothering you — if now's not the right time, I understand. If the timing changes, I'll be here."

**Personalization rules**:
- Reference something specific about their company or role (use `/account-research` to prep)
- Tie your outreach to a business trigger (funding, new hire, expansion, earnings miss)
- Never lead with features — lead with the problem you solve

## Play 3: Stuck Deal — Re-Engage

**When to use**: Deal has been in the same stage for 3+ weeks with no movement.

**Objective**: Get a clear yes, no, or revised timeline — eliminate false pipeline.

**Diagnosis first**: Why is it stuck?
- [ ] Champion has gone quiet → *champion problem*
- [ ] Budget not confirmed → *budget problem*
- [ ] Competing priorities → *urgency problem*
- [ ] Missing EB access → *access problem*
- [ ] Technical concerns unresolved → *evaluation problem*

**Plays by diagnosis**:

**Champion gone quiet**:
- Send a "working assumption" email: "I want to make sure I'm not wasting your time. My working assumption is that [X] is still a priority and you're targeting [date]. If that's still the case, I'd love to reconnect. If priorities have shifted, I'd rather know so I can focus my time appropriately."

**Budget not confirmed**:
- "What would need to happen for budget to be approved for this? Who else needs to be involved in that conversation?"

**Competing priorities**:
- Quantify the cost of delay: "You mentioned [pain] is costing you [amount/time]. At this rate, over the next 90 days that's [math]. Is it worth a conversation about how to get this moving?"

**Missing EB access**:
- Ask the champion directly: "I want to make sure the executive team is aligned before we go further. Can you help me get 20 minutes with [EB name]?"

## Play 4: Competitive Displacement

**When to use**: Prospect is currently using a competitor and you're trying to win the replacement.

**Objective**: Understand why they're unhappy, establish superior value, minimize switching friction.

**Steps**:
1. **Lead with curiosity**: "What made you start looking at alternatives? What is [Competitor] not doing for you?"
2. **Quantify the gap**: "What does that gap cost you? How long has this been a problem?"
3. **Don't attack the incumbent**: "I understand why you chose them — for many companies, that works fine. Where customers move to us is when [specific scenario]."
4. **Address switching friction head-on**: "The #1 reason companies stay with a vendor they're unhappy with is switching cost. Let me walk you through what migration actually looks like — it's usually less painful than people expect."
5. **Reference a migration story**: Use a customer who switched from that competitor.
6. **Proposal**: Include migration support, onboarding plan, timeline to value.

## Play 5: Expansion / Upsell

**When to use**: Existing customer who could expand seat count, add modules, or renew at higher tier.

**Objective**: Expand ARR at renewal or mid-cycle.

**Trigger signals** (watch for these in your accounts):
- Usage growing (more seats actively using than contracted)
- New team or division with same pain
- Customer mentions a pain that maps to a product you haven't sold them yet
- Champion gets promoted or moves to a new team
- Upcoming renewal (90-day window)

**Steps**:
1. **QBR/Business Review**: Lead with value delivered, then pivot to expansion opportunities naturally.
2. **New pain discovery**: "Since we started working together, have new challenges come up in [adjacent area]?"
3. **Stakeholder expansion**: "Is there another team at [Company] that faces a similar challenge? I'd love to help you evangelize internally."
4. **Proposal**: Expansion proposal framed as "here's what year 2 looks like" — not "we want more money."
